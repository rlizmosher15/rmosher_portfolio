Are you a creator? Then you already know what I'm talking about. 

At one point or another in your creative career, you've hit that terrible wall we call creative block. Creative block not only kills productivity, but also stunts your creative flow making it frustrating to work on any projects. 

I'm going to share my top tips that I use when I hit a creative block. 

Art Studies

This may not be the most exciting thing to work on, but working on basic skills is a great way to get into an artistic flow without needing to put too much effort into the actual "creative" aspect. Simply set a goal and execute.

Whether you want to practice drawing hands, expressions, lighting, etc... Simply looking at a reference and drawing with build skills and get you into the flow without having to think too hard. Just choose a reference and start!

Keep a list of personal projects

If you need some inspiration, what better a place than your own little book of ideas! No more sitting and looking at your blank canvas trying to figure out where to start. If you think of a cool idea, add it to the list. Cool dream? List. Took a cool photo and want to paint it? List. 

This will make your life so much easier when trying to figure out what to work on.

Don't work on a white canvas

This is not only helpful for creative block, but can also save your eyes! Blinding white canvases can be intimidating, and make it daunting to start. Try a medium gray, or take a texture brush and make an interesting mash of colors to select from. 

Mix up Mediums

Do you primarily work digitally? Or traditionally? Try switching it up. 

I work primarily digitally, and when nothing is turning out how I want, I'll try sketching in my sketchbook. If I get something that is a good start, I take a picture and import to Photoshop when I'm ready to paint. 

Take a Break!

This is so important! If you are working yourself to death, and are staring blankly at your work not knowing where to go with it... Walk... Away. 

Take your dog for a walk(if you don't have a dog, you should get one...), do some yoga, cook a healthy meal, read a book, or just close your eyes for a few minutes. 

It's so important to take breaks and reset yourself. You'll be surprised at how much better you feel afterward. 

Surround yourself with inspiration

Do you have a favorite artist? Style? Art subject? Something you want to be better at? 

Print out artwork that inspires you and hang it around your work station! This will remind you why you started, or where you're aspiring to go!

Have a Zone... and keep it Clean!!!

Everyone needs a Zone. An office, desk, comfy chair? Whatever your zone, make it an optimal workspace for yourself. Having an area that is comfortable and optimized for working will make such a difference.

Keep your zone clean! Don't leave stray art supplies, trash, or an assortment of projects out. This can be overwhelming and stunt your creative flow. Organize your art supplies(I love Mason Jars for this!). Organize your cables. 

Manage your files

File and digital organization is a great way to clear your head and make it easier to focus on your work, rather than on finding your work. Develop an optimal file heirarchy for yourself and a consistent naming format for all your projects.
