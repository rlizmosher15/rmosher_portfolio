How to Beat Creative Block

For all my fellow creatives out there!

Learn the tips and tricks that I use when I'm in a creative rut

Are you a creator? Then you already know what I'm talking about. 

At one point or another in your creative career, you've hit that terrible wall we call creative block. Creative block not only kills productivity, but also stunts your creative flow making it frustrating to work on any projects. 

I'm going to share my top tips that I use when I hit a creative block. 

Do some Studies

This may not be the most exciting thing to do, but working on basic skills is a great way to get into an artistic flow without needing to put too much effort into the actual "creative" aspect.

Whether you want to practice drawing hands, expressions, lighting, etc... Simply looking at a reference and drawing will build your skills, while getting you into the flow. Just choose a cool reference and get started!

Keep a list of personal projects

If you need some inspiration, what better a place to look than your own little book of ideas! No more sitting and staring at that blank canvas, willing your brain to be cool. If you think of a great idea, add it to the list. Cool dream? List. Took a cool photo and want to paint it? List. 

This will make your life so much easier when your brain is lacking in the interesting ideas department.

Don't work on a white canvas

This tip is not only helpful for creative block, but can also save your eyes!(specifially if you're working digital..) Blinding white canvases can be intimidating, and make it daunting to start. Try switching to a medium gray, or use a texture brush to make an interesting mash of colors.

Mix up Mediums

Do you primarily work digitally? Or traditionally? Try switching it up. 

I work primarily digitally, and when nothing is turning out how I want, I take to my sketchbook. This can help as a warmup, or just a quick brainstorming session. Once I feel like I'm going in the righ direction, I'll take a picture and import to Photoshop to paint. 

Take a Break!

This is so important! If you are working yourself to death, staring blankly at your work and not knowing where to go with it... Walk... Away. 

Take your dog for a walk(if you don't have a dog, you should get one...), do some yoga, cook a healthy meal, read a book, or just close your eyes for a few minutes. 

It's crucial to take breaks and reset yourself. You'll be surprised at how much better you feel afterward. 

Surround yourself with inspiration

Do you have a favorite artist? Style? Art subject? Something you want to be better at? 

Print out artwork that inspires you and hang it around your work station! This will remind you why you started, or where you're aspiring to go!

Have a Zone... and keep it Clean!!!

Everyone needs a Zone. An office, desk, comfy chair? Whatever your zone, make it an optimal workspace for yourself. Having an area that is comfortable and optimized for working will make such a difference.

Keep your zone clean! Don't leave stray art supplies, trash, or an assortment of projects out. This can be overwhelming and stunt your creative flow. Organize your art supplies(I love Mason Jars for this!). Organize your cables. 

Manage your files

File and digital organization is a great way to clear your head and make it easier to focus on your work, rather than on finding your work. Develop an optimal file heirarchy for yourself and a consistent naming format for all your projects.
